**Title:** DEX Arbitrage Explained — Buy Cheap, Sell Dear, in ONE Transaction (Foundry)

The oldest and purest form of MEV: the same token trades at two different prices on two exchanges, and you keep the difference. In this episode we reproduce a real DEX arbitrage between Uniswap and SushiSwap — as a Foundry test against a live fork of Ethereum — and the profit prints for real ($1,331 in one transaction, against real mainnet pools).

Everything runs locally on free tools (Foundry's Anvil), on a private fork — no real users, no real funds.
